## Positioning

The core point:

> Tax preparation records what happened. Tax Membership keeps the tax year from becoming a surprise.

The reader should understand three levels of tax help:

1. `DIY tax software`: You prepare and file the return yourself. The software helps you report, but you are responsible for knowing what matters.
2. `Professional tax preparation`: A professional prepares and files the return accurately. The work is usually backward-looking, document-driven, and concentrated around filing season.
3. `Tax Membership`: The return is still prepared, but the relationship is organized around the full year, with planning touchpoints before the year is over.

## Annual Rhythm

Use a five-window rhythm:

1. `Tax Season: Prepare + Notice`
   - Prepare and file the return.
   - Identify patterns, missed opportunities, withholding issues, estimate problems, and planning themes.
2. `Spring / Early Summer: Reset`
   - Review what the return revealed.
   - Update withholding or estimates.
   - Clarify what changed for the current year.
3. `Summer: Projection`
   - Build a current-year tax projection once the year is taking shape.
   - Review income, bonuses, business profit, retirement contributions, withholdings, estimates, and major changes.
4. `Fall: Strategy`
   - Run planning while there is still time to act.
   - Evaluate retirement contributions, Roth conversions, charitable giving, entity decisions, capital gains/losses, equipment purchases, withholding, and estimate adjustments.
5. `Year-End: Execute`
   - Finalize time-sensitive decisions before December 31.
   - Confirm documents, payments, deadlines, and what needs to happen before filing season.

## Enter-Anytime Rule

The page and one-pager should make clear that clients can enter at any point in the year.

Suggested framing:

- `January-April`: We start with preparation, filing, and identifying planning themes.
- `May-July`: We start by reviewing the filed return and updating withholding or estimates.
- `August-October`: We start with projections and fall planning.
- `November-December`: We start with urgent year-end strategy and setting up the next year properly.

This is one of the strongest reasons to build the asset. It prevents the false impression that someone has to wait until the next tax season to begin.

Core ideas to preserve:
