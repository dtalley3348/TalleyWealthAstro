# Talley Wealth Packaging Infrastructure Plan

Last updated: 2026-06-17

This document translates the growth conversation into a practical packaging system for the Talley Wealth website, proposal workflow, content engine, and referral assets.

The short version: the recent website positioning pass made the front door clearer. Packaging makes the next layer of the site, content, and sales path feel like the same firm with the same point of view.

## Working Analogy

The homepage and main navigation are the front door.

Packaging is the lobby.

The lobby should tell a prospect where to go next without making them decode the whole firm. Someone close to retirement should see the retirement path. A profitable owner should see the business-owner path. Someone asking about Roth conversions should see that the question has a deeper planning home.

Keystone is not the lobby. Keystone is the method underneath the work.

## What Packaging Is

Packaging is the public-facing shape of the work. It answers:

- Who is this for?
- What situation usually makes them care now?
- What problem are they actually trying to solve?
- What decisions need to be coordinated?
- What will Talley Wealth help them figure out?
- How does Keystone turn that situation into a real plan?
- What content, tools, referral assets, and proposal language support the offer?

Packaging should make the market understand the doorway before asking them to understand the whole planning process.

## What Packaging Is Not

Packaging is not a replacement for Keystone.

Packaging is not a promise that Talley Wealth only serves two exact client types.

Packaging is not a reason to rewrite every page into the same formula.

Packaging is not a new service delivery model unless David intentionally chooses to make it one later.

Packaging is the buyer-facing translation layer. Keystone remains the planning engine.

## Recommended Approval Level

Start with Level 1 and a light Level 2.

### Level 1: Website And Content Packaging

This is the lowest-risk and highest-leverage version.

Use the package architecture to organize:

- homepage references
- Planning Focus navigation
- market hubs
- offer pages
- related tools and articles
- internal links
- calls to action
- lead magnets
- video/blog tagging
- COI/referral assets

This does not require changing the actual client experience.

### Level 2: Package-Aware Sales And Proposal Language

This adds a light segmentation layer to the Keystone proposal system.

The Keystone proposal workflow stays intact, but the opening thesis, examples, deliverables, and emphasis should flex based on the prospect's main lane.

This does not require separate proposal systems. It requires one proposal system that knows the prospect's entry point.

### Level 3: Full Operating Model Segmentation

This would segment service calendars, review meetings, onboarding, client communications, and ongoing advisory by market.

Do not start here. This may become valuable later, but it is too much to approve while the packaging idea is still being clarified.

## Core Architecture

The structure should be:

```text
Market
  Public-facing offer
    Decision pages
    Tools and calculators
    Articles and videos
    COI/referral assets
    Email nurture
    Paid/search landing pages
      Keystone Method
        Proposal
        Ongoing advisory, if appropriate
```

In plain English:

- Markets tell people whether the firm is generally built for someone like them.
- Offers tell them what problem Talley Wealth helps solve.
- Decision pages and tools prove the firm understands the real questions underneath the surface question.
- Keystone explains how the work gets done.
- Pricing and proposals explain the engagement.
- Ongoing advisory explains what happens after the initial build.

## Main Packages

The public package architecture has two main market offers:

- Tax-Smart Retirement Planning for the Five Years Before and After Retirement
- Business Owner Planning for Turning Profit Into Personal Wealth

Roth conversion planning is not a separate market package. It is a high-value retirement decision cluster that should live inside the retirement package.

### Package 1: Tax-Smart Retirement Planning

Working name:

Tax-Smart Retirement Planning for the Five Years Before and After Retirement

Primary market:

Pre-retirees, new retirees, and households near the retirement transition.

Buyer trigger:

Retirement is close enough that rough guesses no longer feel good enough.

Plain-English prospect thoughts:

- Can I actually stop working?
- What changes if I wait another year?
- Where does my paycheck come from after work stops?
- What should my 401(k) or IRA be doing now?
- Should I claim Social Security now or later?
- Should I do Roth conversions before the window closes?
- How do Medicare and IRMAA affect the tax plan?
- Would my spouse know what to do if something happened to me?

What Talley Wealth helps coordinate:

- retirement date and work-optional timing
- retirement paycheck design
- tax bracket and Roth conversion windows
- Social Security timing
- Medicare and IRMAA awareness
- withdrawal order across taxable, tax-deferred, and Roth accounts
- investment risk tied to income needs
- estate documents, beneficiaries, and spouse continuity

Current website assets that already support this:

- `/financial-advisor-for-pre-retirees`
- `/services/retirement-planning`
- `/can-i-retire-before-65-tri-cities-tn`
- `/roth-conversions-before-retirement-tri-cities-tn`
- `/resources/learning-center`
- `/resources/tools`
- `/guide`
- `/how-we-work/keystone-method`
- `/pricing`
- `/advisory-fees`

Likely missing or underbuilt assets:

- retirement paycheck page visuals / companion assets
- withdrawal order planning page
- Social Security and Medicare timing page
- IRMAA and retirement tax bracket page
- surviving spouse readiness page
- retirement tax-window checklist
- COI-forwardable "five questions before retirement" PDF/email version
- paid landing page for retirement tax-window planning
- email sequence for people within ten years of retirement

Keystone proposal emphasis:

For a retirement prospect, the proposal should lead with the transition from accumulation to decision sequencing. The organizing thesis should usually reference retirement timing, income, taxes, Roth windows, account order, risk, and spouse continuity.

### Package 2: Business Owner Profit-To-Wealth Planning

Working name:

Business Owner Planning for Turning Profit Into Personal Wealth

Primary market:

Profitable business owners whose business has started to materially shape the household plan.

Buyer trigger:

The business is working, but the owner plan has not caught up.

Plain-English prospect thoughts:

- The business is profitable, so why does my personal plan still feel messy?
- Am I paying myself the right way?
- Is the business building personal wealth or just consuming attention?
- Should I be doing more with retirement plans?
- Is my tax bill a planning signal?
- How much cash should stay in the business versus move to the household?
- What would happen if I sold, slowed down, or had to step away?

What Talley Wealth helps coordinate:

- owner compensation
- distributions, cash reserves, and household cash flow
- business tax strategy
- estimated tax and withholding rhythm
- entity and S-Corp questions, with CPA or attorney coordination when needed
- retirement plan design
- investments outside the business
- concentration risk
- exit, succession, continuity, or sale readiness
- estate and family planning around business wealth

- `/pricing`
- `/advisory-fees`

Likely missing or underbuilt assets:

- owner pay planning companion visuals / deeper support assets

- business cash-to-household wealth page
- owner liquidity and reserves page
- exit readiness or "what if I sell" planning page
- business owner year-end tax planning checklist
- COI-forwardable owner planning one-pager
- CPA/attorney referral version of owner planning

Keystone proposal emphasis:

For a business owner prospect, the proposal should lead with the fact that the business and household are not separate financial projects. The organizing thesis should usually reference owner pay, tax strategy, business liquidity, retirement plans, investments outside the business, and eventual independence from the business.

### Retirement Decision Cluster: Roth Conversion And Retirement Tax Window Planning

Working name:

Roth Conversion and Retirement Tax Window Planning

Primary role:

High-intent retirement decision cluster. This is not a separate market. It is one of the most important retirement tax-window topics and should route into the broader retirement package.

Buyer trigger:

The person keeps hearing about Roth conversions and realizes the question is too expensive to guess at.

Plain-English prospect thoughts:

- Should I do Roth conversions?
- How much should I convert?
- What tax bracket should I fill?
- Should I wait until after retirement?
- How do RMDs, Social Security, Medicare, and future tax rates change the answer?
- Is the goal to lower this year's taxes or lifetime taxes?

What Talley Wealth helps coordinate:

- multi-year tax projection
- conversion pacing
- federal bracket room
- IRMAA and Medicare thresholds
- RMD pressure
- Social Security taxation
- cash available to pay conversion tax
- investment allocation and asset location
- estate and beneficiary goals

Current website assets that already support this:

- `/roth-conversions-before-retirement-tri-cities-tn`
- `/resources/tools`
- Roth Conversion Threshold Visual
- `/services/tax-planning`
- `/services/retirement-planning`
- `/financial-advisor-for-pre-retirees`
- `/how-we-work/keystone-method`

Likely missing or underbuilt assets:

- Roth conversion basics page
- year-by-year Roth conversion planning page
- IRMAA/Roth conversion interaction page
- RMD and Roth conversion page
- Roth conversion bad-fit page or FAQ
- Roth conversion checklist or mini-guide

- COI-forwardable "Roth conversions are a pacing decision" asset

Keystone proposal emphasis:

For a Roth-led prospect, the proposal should not imply the whole engagement is only a Roth calculation. It should say the Roth question is the doorway, but the answer depends on retirement income, taxes, Medicare, account order, investments, and estate goals.

## Keystone's Role

Keystone should stay public and visible.

The shift is role clarity:

- Planning Focus pages answer "is this firm for someone like me?"
- Offer pages answer "do they understand the problem I want help with?"
- Keystone answers "how does the work actually get done?"
- Pricing answers "what does the engagement cost and what am I buying?"
- Ongoing Advisory answers "what happens after the initial plan is built?"

The Keystone page should not have to carry the whole burden of explaining the market fit. It should be the method layer after the prospect recognizes the relevant situation.

Decision pages should route back into the package they support.

They are good for SEO, AI-search, social repurposing, and paid landing page testing.

### Tools And Calculators

Tools should act like proof that the firm understands the decision shape.

Tools should be attached to packages:

- Retirement tools
- Roth/tax-window tools

The tools page can be broad, but each tool should have a primary package tag and related pages.

### Resources And Learning Center

The content library should be organized by package tags, not just generic categories.

Every article, video, and FAQ should carry:

- primary package

- content type
- intended distribution channel
- CTA destination

## Proposal Workflow Implications

The existing Keystone proposal system should not be replaced.

Add a package-aware layer.

### Proposed Proposal Fields

Every proposal should identify, when source evidence supports it:

- primary package, or `unclear` if no primary lane is apparent
- secondary package or decision cluster, if any
- entry question
- trigger event
- core thesis
- package/persona fit notes
- Keystone tier
- main deliverable emphasis
- important exclusions or fit boundaries

Do not force a package or persona. Roth/tax-window planning is a retirement decision cluster or entry question, not a primary package. Use it as secondary context only when the broader retirement context is actually evident.

Package-aware does not mean persona-stamped. Use the parts of the language that fit and leave out the parts that would overstate the situation. A solopreneur attorney may need owner-pay, tax, cash-flow, and personal-wealth language without sounding like a multi-partner medical practice with staff, payroll, and succession complexity.

### Proposal Thesis Examples

Retirement:

The issue is not simply whether you have enough. The issue is how retirement timing, income, taxes, Social Security, Medicare, portfolio risk, and spouse continuity should be coordinated before the decisions start narrowing.

Business owner:

The business is now shaping the household plan. The Keystone work should connect owner pay, tax strategy, business cash flow, retirement plan design, investments outside the business, and the eventual path toward personal wealth that does not depend entirely on the company.

Roth/tax window:

The Roth conversion question is the doorway, not the whole job. The right answer depends on tax brackets, retirement income, Medicare thresholds, RMD pressure, investment location, cash flow, and estate goals over multiple years.

### Proposal System Change Scope

Near term:

- Add optional package classification and fit notes to proposal intake, including an explicit unclear/no-forced-package option.
- Add package-specific opening thesis language only when the evidence supports the package.
- Add package-specific deliverable emphasis only when it fits the prospect's actual situation.
- Keep Keystone pricing and engagement structure stable.

Later:

- Create package-specific proposal modules.
- Create package-specific follow-up email variants.
- Create package-specific internal handoff checklists.
- Tie proposal outputs to content assets the prospect can read after the call.

## Content Engine Interface

The video-first content engine should feed the packaging system.

Raw video should not become random content. Every asset should be assigned to a package before it is turned into pages, posts, or emails.

### Minimum Metadata For Every Video

- raw video filename
- package
- entry question
- audience
- source prompt
- transcript
- best quote or hook
- recommended output types
- approval status
- final destination URLs, once published

### Recommended Output Types

From one good video:

- short social caption
- LinkedIn post
- blog or Learning Center draft
- landing page section
- email snippet
- COI/referral forwardable note
- FAQ entry
- internal sales/proposal language snippet

### Approval Gate

Start with production to an approval queue, not direct auto-posting.

The machine should create drafts and renderable clips. David should approve anything that speaks for the firm.

## COI And Referral Layer

COI/referral distribution is a curated subset of the content engine.

It should use the same package architecture, but with different packaging.

A COI asset should answer:

- What situation should make the COI think of Talley Wealth?
- What client language should they listen for?
- What short asset can they forward?
- What is the least awkward next step?

Example retirement COI asset:

Title:

Five Retirement Questions Clients Should Answer Before They Stop Working

COI listener phrases:

- "I think I am close, but I am not sure."
- "I do not know where the paycheck comes from."
- "I keep hearing about Roth conversions."
- "I am not sure when to claim Social Security."
- "My spouse would be lost if something happened to me."

Forwardable note:

David put together a plain-English guide on the questions that tend to matter most in the years right around retirement. This might be useful if you are starting to think through timing, taxes, income, and what needs to be organized before work fully stops.

## Internal Linking Rules

Use these rules when building or updating pages:

- Retirement pages should link to the retirement offer page, Roth/tax-window content when relevant, tools, Keystone, and pricing.
- Business owner pages should link to the owner offer page, S-Corp/business tax content when relevant, tools, Keystone Owner language, and pricing.
- Roth pages should link to retirement planning, tax planning, the Roth tool, Keystone, and pricing.
- Keystone should link back to the main offers instead of standing alone as an abstract method.
- Pricing should make clear that Keystone is the engagement and the packages are the situations Keystone is built to solve.
- Ongoing Advisory should explain how package-specific issues continue after Keystone.
