This audit keeps the local SEO buildout from turning into thin city-service duplicates. The goal is not to create the most URLs. The goal is to keep the URLs that have a clear search intent, a specific local or persona angle, and enough substance to deserve indexation.

## Existing City-Service Pages

Some city-service pages are not automatically bad. The pages worth keeping are the ones where the local modifier changes the actual advice.

Examples that currently have a reasonable reason to exist:

- Kingsport retirement and tax pages because Eastman benefits create a specific planning context.
- Bristol tax page because the TN/VA state line creates a specific search intent.
- Sevierville business/tax pages because short-term rental and tourism business issues are distinct.
- Asheville and Knoxville pages may be useful, but should stay secondary until Johnson City, Bristol, and Kingsport are stronger.

## Review Signal

Talley Wealth has a major local trust advantage: 67 public Google reviews with a 5.0 average rating at the time this audit was written. The site should use this as local proof, but in a compliance-safe way:

- Prefer aggregate proof and links to public review sources.
- Avoid promising outcomes.
- Avoid cherry-picked testimonial claims unless Cambridge approves the exact usage and disclosures.
- Keep disclosures near review proof where practical.

## Next Content Rule

Do not create a new local service URL unless the page can answer a different human question than the city hub and the canonical service page. If a proposed page cannot clear that bar, redirect or fold it into the stronger canonical page.
