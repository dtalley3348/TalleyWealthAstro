# Content Pipeline Operating Brief

Last updated: June 30, 2026

This brief defines what the Talley Wealth content pipeline is trying to create. It is the strategy layer above the scripts: who the content is for, how ideas should be transformed, what "good" looks like, and where compliance gates sit.

## Primary Audience Lanes

The pipeline should keep returning to a small set of durable audience lanes instead of chasing random topics.

- Pre-retirees and near-retirees who want confidence before making work optional.
- Business owners whose tax, cash flow, business, and personal wealth decisions are connected.
- High-income professionals with complexity, equity, taxes, retirement decisions, charitable intent, or planning fatigue.
- Inherited-wealth clients who are dealing with both decisions and emotion.
- Successful families who already have professionals but still lack coordination.
- Referral partners who need to understand the kind of complexity Talley Wealth is built for.

The best content usually names a problem the right person has felt but may not have known how to describe: the question behind the question, the coordination gap, the fear under a financial decision, or the planning sequence that makes one decision affect another.

## 90-Day Editorial Starting Mix

Use this as the starting mix for recording prompts and source-material capture, not as a rigid posting quota. Adjust it based on what David actually records, what gets approved, what is already scheduled, and which clips become strong reuse candidates.

- 35% retirement transition: retirement paycheck, confidence, tax windows, Roth/RMD/IRMAA, Social Security, spending, spouse continuity, and the question of whether work can really become optional.
- 25% business owner profit-to-wealth: owner pay, cash flow, year-round tax planning, personal wealth outside the business, the business as asset versus the business as job, and exit pressure.
- 15% coordination and implementation: CPA/advisor/attorney gaps, Keystone-style sequencing, advice that does not get implemented, and the need for someone to own the whole picture.
- 10% local/regional/real-life market: Johnson City, the Tri-Cities, regional employers, local owners, local retirees, community trust, and the practical world clients actually live in. This should not feel like generic local SEO content.
- 10% secondary complexity: high-income W-2 professionals, healthcare professionals, equity compensation, inherited wealth, charitable/estate decisions, and successful families with too many moving parts.
- 5% David POV/referral partner: plain-English beliefs, blunt observations, CPA/attorney-forward clips, and ideas a smart referral partner could forward without needing to explain Talley Wealth.

This mix should keep Talley Wealth feeling like the parent judgment brand, not a leftover channel after the narrower surfaces receive their topics.

## Package Architecture

Content should reinforce the public Talley Wealth service architecture.

- Explore Call: the low-friction first conversation. The goal is fit, not closing.
- Keystone Method: concentrated planning work. It is where decisions are clarified, the moving parts are brought into one view, and implementation begins.
- Ongoing Advisory: the structure for keeping taxes, investments, retirement income, estate planning, charitable giving, insurance, cash flow, business decisions, and life changes connected over time.
- Talley Tax: a related but separate tax business. When content crosses into tax-specific work, use the appropriate separate-business and non-affiliation language.

The content should not make Keystone feel like a generic planning package. Keystone is not a binder, a product pitch, or a one-time consultation. It is concentrated planning work led by David, with implementation ownership.

## Brand And Surface Roles

Choose the story first, then route it. Do not let a surface need produce generic filler.

- Talley Wealth is the main house. It should receive retirement, business-owner, coordination, local, Keystone, and David judgment content. It should feel like the full planning brain.
- Retire With Talley is a focused retirement-transition surface. It can receive adapted retirement clips, but it should not siphon all retirement thinking away from Talley Wealth.
- Talley Tax receives tax-specific education when the topic truly belongs to the separate tax business or tax-planning audience. Not every tax-smart planning thought belongs there.
- David Personal receives higher-trust POV, founder voice, local credibility, business/AI/strategy-adjacent thought leadership, and occasional planning philosophy.

Daily recording prompts should know what job they are doing in this system before they ask David to record.

## Core Point Of View

These beliefs should recur across videos, posts, blogs, pages, emails, carousels, and one-pagers.

- David's judgment is the asset. The video is only the container.
- The real problem is often not the first problem named.
- Confidence comes from understanding, not from being handed an answer.
- The plan dictates the assets, not the other way around.
- The goal is not less risk by default; it is the right risk calibrated to the plan and person.
- Tax planning should be year-round and lifetime-aware, not only this year's tax bill.
- Competent professionals can still leave the client uncoordinated.
- Implementation is where planning becomes real.
- Successful people often need judgment more than more information.
- Complexity is not a personal failure.
- Business owners usually need a strategy partner, not only a tax saver.
- Keystone is not for everyone, and that filter is part of the design.

## Voice Rules

Write as David explaining something across a conference table to someone he genuinely cares about.

- Warm, direct, grounded, plain-English.
- Calm confidence, not hype.
- Willing to say what is not a fit.
- Specific enough that the right person recognizes themselves.
- First person is allowed when it conveys judgment or lived advisory experience.
- Preserve David's sharp source lines instead of sanding them into generic marketing.
- Use prose by default. Use bullets only when they improve scanning, decisions, or next steps.
- Avoid influencer voice, fake urgency, advisor-bashing, or over-polished slogans.
- Avoid AI-ish density and workshop language.
- Avoid hard-sell CTAs. Prefer invitations.
- Avoid clever metaphor closes or polished campaign lines that David would not actually say out loud.

Hard bans and strong avoids:

- No "fee-only."
- No em dashes or exclamation points.
- Avoid: holistic, synergy, roadmap, financial journey, unlock, empower, optimize, solutions, peace of mind, deep dive, leverage, elevate, transform, game-changer.
- Avoid guaranteed outcomes, best-advisor claims, market-beating claims, risk-elimination claims, and employer endorsements.

Useful phrases:

- "real financial planning"
- "plain-English"
- "the plan tells us what the investments need to do"
- "the plan dictates the assets"
- "decisions that affect each other"
- "understanding leads to confidence"
- "someone has to own implementation"

## Compliance Posture

The pipeline is review-gated. No asset should post, publish, upload, or send until it is approved through the intended local review workflow.

- Social posts are drafted locally and queued for David's approval before scheduling.
- YouTube stays out of the normal short-form quick-add workflow. When a video becomes part of an approved Resource Publishing package, the package step may queue the final resource video to YouTube through Metricool because that same video is entering the compliance review path.
- Blog posts and website pages may be staged locally, but Cambridge PDF approval is required before publishing live.
- PDFs for compliance should be generated from the staged webpage view when possible, so compliance sees the page as it will appear.
- Disclosures should be humanized, not pasted in as awkward boilerplate.
- Client examples should be hypothetical or representative unless there is specific permission and the required disclosure path.

Default social disclaimer:

> This is general education, not advice for your specific situation.

Default planning/tax/estate disclaimer pattern:

> For discussion purposes only. This is not individualized legal or tax advice. Estate, tax, and investment decisions should be reviewed against the full facts and with the right professional before implementation.

Use hedging verbs where appropriate: may, might, could, can help evaluate, can model, can coordinate.

## Content Reuse Model

One transcript should be treated as a source asset, not as a single post. The pipeline should decide how much reuse the idea deserves.

Triage levels:

- Post Only: quick thought, no durable search value. Usually social captions only.
- Caption / Post: one useful idea worth a clip, platform captions, and possibly a carousel.
- Website Asset: evergreen answer someone might search. Add blog draft and internal links.
- Core POV Asset: foundational David/Talley Wealth belief. Add blog draft, possible page/one-pager reuse, and flag as reusable language.

Default outputs from a strong talking-head clip:

- Captioned vertical video.
- Clean reframed videos for platform use.
- Open-eyes smart cover, defaulting to David's face when possible.
- Social pack for LinkedIn, Instagram, Facebook, Google Business Profile, and X.
- X-native thread and, when the idea is strong enough, extra X-native short posts.
- Carousel slides for swipeable education.
- Content log and approval note.
- Blog draft only when the transcript has evergreen depth.
- Blog PDF after the staged page exists and before live publication.
- Optional one-pager or email when the topic maps to a core offer, guide, referral-partner explanation, or high-value planning theme.

The durable reuse layer should preserve ideas even when they are not drafted immediately. Each processed video should feed a content index and reuse queue so future blog, FAQ, one-pager, email, landing-page brief, and website-snippet opportunities can be searched, reviewed, and generated locally later.

The reuse queue is not a publishing queue. It is a judgment queue. It should make promising ideas visible without pretending every transcript deserves a permanent page or long-form asset.

## Platform Fit

The same idea should not be mechanically pasted everywhere. It should be translated to the job of each platform.

- LinkedIn: judgment, professional credibility, a little more context, low-pressure CTA.
- Instagram: strongest hook, simple teaching, carousel/video fit, save/share value.
- Facebook: warm explanation, local trust, clear educational tone.
- Google Business Profile: local SEO, short educational post, Northeast Tennessee relevance when natural.
- X: higher-frequency text-native thinking. Use threads for bigger ideas, but also produce short standalone takes, contrarian observations, quote posts, and replies-to-self angles from the same source.
- YouTube: Resource Publishing companion only by default. Use the final horizontal resource video, stronger title/description, and an article link. Do not automatically create Shorts from normal short-form approvals.
- Blog: search-intent answer and internal-link hub.
- Email: selective planning note, not one email per video.

## X Cadence Rule

X should usually post more often than the other platforms, but that does not mean every video asset should become more videos on X.

Reconcile the gap this way:

- Keep the main X asset as a short thread when the idea needs sequence.
- Add 2 to 4 extra text-native X posts from the same transcript when there are distinct angles.
- Space those extra X posts away from the thread and each other.
- Do not duplicate the same wording from LinkedIn or the carousel caption.
- Use X for crisp beliefs, one-line frameworks, misconception corrections, quote-style David lines, and "the question behind the question" observations.
- If the transcript is thin, do not force extra X posts just to meet volume.

In other words: X gets more frequency because it gets more text-native derivatives, not because every other platform's asset count should rise.

## What Counts As A Good Output

A good output should:

- Sound like David, not like generic financial content.
- Make one useful judgment visible.
- Name the deeper tension behind the surface topic.
- Fit one of the audience lanes.
- Reinforce the package architecture without forcing a pitch.
- Preserve compliance discipline.
- Give the reader/viewer a reason to trust David's thinking.
- Produce reviewable assets without David needing to run commands.
- Make it obvious what gets posted, what is held, what needs compliance review, and what is only a draft.

A weak output:

- Explains a generic financial topic anyone could write.
- Over-polishes David's voice into marketing mush.
- Gives unqualified advice or implies guaranteed outcomes.
- Creates too many assets from a thin transcript.
- Uses the same caption everywhere without platform fit.
- Publishes or schedules without the approval boundary.

## Transcript-To-Asset Rules

Before drafting from a transcript, identify:

- The surface question.
- The deeper problem.
- The belief being challenged.
- The audience lane.
- The one-sentence thesis.
- The strongest David line to preserve.
- The compliance risk, if any.

Then route the transcript:

- Social posts: preserve one clean idea, tailor by platform, keep the disclaimer.
- X posts: make a thread plus short text-native derivatives when the idea supports it.
- Blog posts: only for Website/Core assets; use `BLOG-RESOURCE-BLUEPRINT.md`.
  Answer the search question early, name the deeper problem, add a decision
  framework and practical added-value block, add local/client-fit context,
  internal links, balanced risks/limits, and a compliance-safe CTA.
- Pages: only for core offer, persona, guide, or POV material that deserves a durable home.
- One-pagers: use for referral-partner explanations, Keystone fit, retirement transition themes, business-owner planning themes, or repeat client education.
- Emails: use sparingly. Convert only the strongest Website/Core assets into a plain-English planning note.

The goal is not maximum asset count. The goal is the right number of high-trust assets from each idea.
