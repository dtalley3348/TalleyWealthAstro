# Compliance Content Workflow

## Known Compliance Context
David is an IAR working with an RIA and broker/dealer. Social video is monitored and archived by SMARSH. Long-form video and website copy need PDF submission to Cambridge Investment Research for compliance approval before going live.

## Working Assumptions
All public website and long-form content should avoid:
- Promissory language.
- Performance claims.
- Implied guarantees of tax savings or investment results.
- Misleading testimonials or cherry-picked outcomes.
- Unsubstantiated superiority claims.
- Personalized advice without context.
- Any language that could imply outcomes are typical.

## Preferred Language Patterns
Use:
- may, might, could, can help evaluate, can model, can coordinate.
- representative situation, not a guarantee.
- planning strategy, tradeoffs, coordination, decision support.
- fiduciary standard where accurate and appropriately scoped.

Avoid:
- will save you taxes.
- guaranteed retirement confidence.
- best advisor.
- beats the market.
- eliminates risk.
- client success implies expected outcome.

## Approval Workflow
For website pages and long-form videos:
1. Draft content.
2. Export compliance PDF.
3. Submit to Cambridge.
4. Track approval status.
5. Publish only after approval.
6. Record final URL and publish date.

## Content Log Fields
- Content title.
- Format: page, article, long video, short video, GBP post, email.
- Target audience.
- Target query or topic.
- Claims requiring review.
- Disclosures used.
- Approval status.
- Approval date.
- Published URL.
- Archive location.

## Implementation Note
Before major website SEO pages go live, generate PDFs for review. Draft pages can be built locally, but publishing should wait for the normal approval process.
