## 1. Pinned Intro

Suggested use: pinned post on X and/or LinkedIn.

```text
Most financial questions have a better question one layer deeper.

"Can I retire?" is also a question about time, family, taxes, healthcare, risk, and identity.

"How much should I pay myself?" is also a question about taxes, business cash, personal wealth, and how much of your life is still tied to the company.

"Should I do a Roth conversion?" is usually a question about a tax window, not just a tax tactic.

That is the kind of planning I tend to write about here.

I am David Talley, founder of Talley Wealth and Talley Tax. CFP(R), ChFC(R), EA.
```

## 2. Better Questions

```text
Most people ask better financial questions after you slow the first question down.

"Can I retire?" becomes "What would I need to feel confident spending?"

"How much should I pay myself?" becomes "What should still be tied to the business?"

The better question is usually one layer deeper.
```

## 3. AI And Judgment

```text
AI is going to make a lot of work faster.

That does not make judgment less valuable. It makes judgment more obvious.

The advantage is not just producing more. It is knowing what should exist, what should not, and what still needs a human point of view.
```

## 4. Systems That Make Work Better

```text
The best systems do not remove responsibility.

They make responsibility easier to carry.

That is true in a financial plan, a tax process, a content engine, or a team workflow. The point is not automation for its own sake. The point is fewer dropped balls and better decisions.
```

## 5. Founder POV

```text
One thing I have learned building a firm:

The work gets better when the message gets sharper.

Not because you stop helping everyone else, but because the right people finally understand why the work was built for them.
```

## 6. Business Owner Wealth

```text
A profitable business can make someone feel wealthy and trapped at the same time.

The money is there, but too much of the family's life is still tied to one private company.

At some point, the question becomes how to turn profit into personal wealth without losing what made the business work.
```

## 7. Retirement Confidence

```text
A lot of retirement anxiety is not really about whether a spreadsheet says the plan works.

It is about not knowing where the paycheck comes from, what changes first, and what happens if the market or tax picture moves at the wrong time.

Confidence usually needs a framework, not just a number.
```

## 8. Tax Strategy

```text
Tax strategy is rarely about one clever move.

More often, it is about seeing the decision before the deadline takes it away.

The best tax planning usually feels obvious after the fact. The hard part is seeing it early enough.
```

## 9. Leadership And Clarity

```text
A leader's job is often translation.

Take something complicated enough to make smart people freeze, and make the next right move visible.

That applies to money, teams, AI, and almost every meaningful system I have worked on.
```
