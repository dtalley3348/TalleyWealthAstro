# Keystone Method

This is the current working guidance for talking about Keystone in client deliverables and internal tools.

## Core Idea

Keystone is concentrated planning work. It is not a binder, a product pitch, or a one-time consultation. It is the period where David leads the work, the moving parts are brought into one view, decisions are made, and the actions those decisions require get moving.

The current website remains the public source of truth for Keystone language and structure.

## End-Date Philosophy

Keystone has an operational six-month structure, but client-facing language should not make it feel like a hard stop.

Use language like:

- as the concentrated Keystone work starts turning into the next chapter
- as we move from building the plan to keeping it aligned
- at this stage of Keystone
- the next chapter

Avoid language like:

- Keystone ends
- graduation
- final closeout
- final meeting, unless it is literally the final scheduled meeting
- after Keystone is complete

## Keystone Summary Purpose

The Keystone Summary should make the value of Keystone visible without becoming a giant plan document. It should show what was decided, why it mattered, what is in motion, what still needs attention, and how the work should be kept coordinated from here.

It should help the client see:

- what changed
- what decisions were made
- what actions are already moving
- what still deserves attention
- why ongoing advisory is the natural structure for keeping the plan aligned

## Deliverable Workflow

The repeatable system is the thinking sequence, not a fixed set of section titles.

1. Gather the source packet from Slant and Google Drive by default.
2. Create an internal interpretation brief before client-facing writing.
3. Answer the seven questions in `tools/keystone-deliverables/spec.md`.
4. Let David correct the interpretation in plain language.
5. Choose the client-facing structure only after the interpretation is clear.

The final deliverable should feel uniquely organized around the client. One client may need the plan explained through account jobs. Another may need guardrails, retirement income buckets, family stewardship, business-exit decisions, tax windows, or charitable intent. Do not force the same structure across clients.

## Reasoning Requirement

Use the strongest reasoning-capable model/setting available when creating the pre-draft interpretation brief and first client-facing draft. This is judgment work, not formatting work.

The right workflow is:

1. Read Slant, Google Drive, and Keystone source material.
2. Produce a pre-draft interpretation brief.
3. Invite David to ramble corrections, nuance, and objections.
4. Convert that into a client-ready draft.
5. Use faster modes only for mechanical polish after the interpretation is right.

## Ongoing Advisory Framing

Ongoing advisory is not just checking in on investments. It is the structure for keeping taxes, investments, retirement income, estate planning, charitable giving, insurance, cash flow, and life changes connected over time.

Use David's core idea: the plan comes before investment management or tax prep.

For investment-heavy clients, this also means the plan dictates the assets, not the other way around.
