# Two-Lane Marketing System

Last updated: June 4, 2026

## Purpose

Use the two primary lanes to guide video prompts, referral assets, lead magnets, email follow-up, and retargeting.

Central line:

Talley Wealth helps people make clearer decisions when retirement, taxes, investments, and business strategy all start affecting each other.

## Primary Lane 1: Tax-Smart Retirement In Northeast Tennessee

Audience:

- Households approaching retirement, usually within five to ten years of leaving full-time work.
- Often $1M-$5M in investable assets.
- Usually local or regionally connected to Johnson City, Kingsport, Bristol, or Northeast Tennessee.

Core emotional problem:

- "We have saved, but we do not know whether the next chapter actually works."

Lead-magnet direction:

- Working title: Tax-Smart Retirement Planning In Northeast Tennessee.
- Core sections: retirement timing, income sequencing, Roth conversion windows, Social Security, Medicare/IRMAA, investment risk, estate coordination, and year-by-year decisions before retirement.
- CTA: Schedule an Explore Call to talk through the retirement question that feels most unresolved.

Referral angle:

- Send to clients nearing retirement or clients who have friends wondering if they can retire.
- Position it as a useful planning framework, not a sales brochure.

Video prompt rules:

- Start with a real scene: a couple five years from retirement, a large IRA balance, a Social Security timing question, a Roth conversion suggestion that no one has connected to Medicare, or a spouse who wants confidence before work stops.
- The deeper problem should usually be sequencing, tax timing, confidence through understanding, or plan-before-portfolio.

## Primary Lane 2: Business Owner Tax Strategy And Personal Wealth

Audience:

- Business owners whose profits, tax bills, owner compensation, cash flow, entity structure, retirement plan, or exit questions have grown more consequential.
- They are tired of professionals who only speak one language: markets, tax filing, legal documents, or insurance.

Core emotional problem:

- "The business is working, but I am not sure it is turning into personal wealth in the smartest way."

Lead-magnet direction:

- Working title: Turning Business Profit Into Personal Wealth.
- Core sections: owner compensation, estimated taxes, retirement plan design, entity decisions, cash flow reserves, investment strategy outside the business, exit readiness, and coordinating CPA/attorney/advisor decisions.
- CTA: Schedule an Explore Call to talk through the business/tax question that keeps resurfacing.

Referral angle:

- Send to owner-clients and referral partners as a useful framework they can forward to other owners.
- Use plain language: "This is the kind of guide I wish more profitable owners had before tax season."

Video prompt rules:

- Start with a real scene: a painful tax bill, a profitable year with little personal wealth to show for it, a CPA who explains the return but not the strategy, an owner wondering whether to hire family, fund a plan, change comp, or prepare for a sale.
- The deeper problem should usually be business/personal integration, tax timing, owner compensation, or no one owning the whole strategy.

## Kitces-Inspired Client Survey Workflow

Use this only with clients where trust is already strong.

Meeting format:

1. Invite the client to a short feedback conversation.
2. Ask what originally made them choose Talley Wealth.
3. Ask what has been most valuable after working together.
4. Ask how they would explain the work to someone else.
5. Ask what would make the experience better.
6. In the final few minutes, say Talley Wealth is especially focused on two kinds of families: people nearing retirement and business owners with tax/strategy complexity.
7. Ask whether anyone comes to mind who is facing one of those situations.

Follow-up:

- Send a brief meeting summary.
- Include the relevant flagship resource once available.
- Send a handwritten thank-you card.
- If the client named a possible referral, follow up after two weeks.

Compliance guardrails:

- Do not compensate clients for referrals.
- Do not imply client experiences guarantee future results.
- Keep the request framed as an introduction to someone who may benefit from a conversation.

## Retargeting And Email Priority

Retargeting audiences should be split by landing-page intent:

- Retirement pages and retirement guide visitors.
- Business-owner and tax-planning visitors.
- General site visitors.

Email nurture should use the same lanes:

- Retirement sequence: confidence, sequencing, tax windows, income, Social Security/Medicare, Explore Call.
- Business-owner sequence: tax pain, owner comp, business/personal separation, retirement plan design, exit optionality, Explore Call.
- General sequence: Talley Wealth philosophy, Keystone, pricing, reviews, Explore Call.
