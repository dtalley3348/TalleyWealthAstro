# What life questions should I ask myself before retiring?

Retirement planning often focuses on the numbers, investments, taxes, making sure the financial side works out. That matters. But the hardest question, and sometimes the most important, is what you actually want your life to look like once work is done.

I have sat across the table from people who spent their whole careers building something, only to realize they have not really asked themselves: What will my days actually look like when I retire? Sometimes they have a to-do list or a few plans, but when we talk it through, it becomes clear that they have not spent much time picturing their real daily life on the other side of retirement. For some, this is the first time they've been asked, and it can be a meaningful, even emotional, conversation.

It is common for people to either avoid the question, shrug it off, or feel lost because their whole identity has been wrapped up in their work. For others, it sneaks up slowly. Either way, it is a real, and underappreciated, part of preparing for retirement.

**Life questions to ask yourself before retiring:**

- What will I do with my days, not just for the first month, but in the long run?
- Where will I find purpose or meaning when work emails and meetings stop?
- What do I want to still contribute, learn, or experience?
- Who do I want to spend more time with? Are there relationships I want to invest in?
- How much structure do I need in my days to feel good?
- What, if anything, do I want to avoid, a loss of momentum, boredom, isolation?
- Have I talked honestly with my spouse or family about what this transition could mean for both of us?

It is easy for the financial plan to take center stage, but these questions are just as important. When you are confident in both your numbers and your vision for life after work, your retirement plan becomes a lot more real.

If you want help thinking through these questions, not just the math, this is a conversation I have often.
