## Source Lines

- The right investment decisions start with knowing your time horizon and your real tolerance for volatility—then deciding what job your money needs to do. The rate of return is an output, not a target. Framing the question this way may lead to better portfolio decisions.
- Most people are asking for a single number without understanding the bigger sequence of decisions that determines what return is even relevant, or what their plan and risk profile require.
- What rate of return should I have on my investments?
- Don’t start with rate of return. Start with time horizon and how much swing you can handle.
- The most common question I get is, 'What rate of return should I look for?' It sounds simple, but skips key steps.
- If you start your investment plan by asking about return targets, you skip the work that actually matters.
- "First, decide what job each dollar needs to do before worrying about the return." , David Talley
