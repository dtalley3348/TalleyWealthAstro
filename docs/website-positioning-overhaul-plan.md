# Website Positioning Overhaul Plan

## Objective

Make the first layer of the Talley Wealth site sharper without rewriting the entire site.

The new public front door should make it obvious that the firm is built around:

1. Tax-smart retirement planning for the five years before and after retirement.
2. Business owner planning for turning profit into personal wealth.

Roth conversion and retirement tax-window planning should remain a high-interest retirement decision cluster, not a separate market beside the two core client situations.

Secondary audiences remain legitimate entry points when the situation needs deeper planning, but they should not compete with the core positioning in the header, homepage, or main conversion pages.

## Voice Guardrails

- Keep David's existing plain-English voice.
- Prefer real decision pressure over abstract positioning.
- Avoid generic advisor phrases like "comprehensive solutions," "financial confidence," "tailored strategies," and "holistic wealth management."
- Avoid leaning on "connected planning" as a default phrase.
- Use specific language: retirement dates, tax windows, Roth conversions, Medicare thresholds, Social Security, owner pay, business profit, cash flow, retirement plans, estate documents, and investment allocation.
- Do not imply tax savings are guaranteed.
- Do not describe Talley Wealth as fee-only.

## Scope

Heavy edits:

- Header and mobile navigation.
- Footer link architecture.
- Homepage.
- Who We Help page.

Moderate edits:

- Keystone Method hero/fit language.
- Pricing hero and "planning comes first" language.
- Services page framing.
- Explore Call page hero and fit list.
- Core persona titles/descriptions where they appear in generated pages.

Light edits:

- Secondary audience pages only where they need to route back into the revised architecture.
- Decision-cluster pages should mostly stay intact because many already carry the strongest specific language.

## Review Standard

After implementation, review the site in this order:

1. Homepage.
2. Header navigation and mobile menu.
3. Who We Help.
4. Keystone Method.
5. Pricing.
6. Services.
7. Explore Call.
8. Pre-retiree, business-owner, and Roth conversion decision pages.

The final handoff should rank which updates feel strongest and where David's opinion is most useful.
