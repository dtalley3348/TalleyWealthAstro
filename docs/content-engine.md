# Video-Led Content Engine

## Strategic Advantage
David is comfortable recording short-form and medium-form educational videos. That should become the raw material for SEO, AI visibility, social reach, YouTube search, Google Business Profile posts, email, and blog content.

## Core Workflow
The best inputs start with a specific viewer, not a generic topic.

Default recording model:

**micro-audience -> real situation -> deeper problem -> turn -> talk-through**

One recorded answer can become:

- YouTube title, description, chapters if long enough.
- Instagram/short-form caption.
- Website article or FAQ page section.
- Internal links to relevant service, city, and persona pages.
- Google Business Profile post.
- Newsletter/email snippet.
- Compliance archive item.

After Mallory edits a batch and transcripts are available, triage each transcript:

- **Post Only:** publish the video without major repurposing.
- **Caption/Post:** create a caption, LinkedIn post, email snippet, or short written post.
- **Website Asset:** turn the strongest explanation into a Learning Center Q&A, blog section, FAQ, or support copy.
- **Core POV Asset:** preserve strong David lines/themes for future hooks, website copy, emails, and messaging.

Final video titles should usually come from the edited video and transcript. Pre-recording titles are working labels only.

## Best Video Formats
- Question and answer: 30 seconds to 3 minutes.
- Short story: 60 seconds to 5 minutes.
- Whiteboard explanation: 2 to 10 minutes.
- Myth correction: 30 to 90 seconds.
- Localized answer: 45 seconds to 3 minutes.

## Publishing Cadence
Recommended starting point:
- 1 strong search-intent answer per day.
- Optional 1-2 lighter clips per day if quality stays high.
- 1 weekly longer video or whiteboard explanation.
- 2-3 website articles per week created from the strongest videos.

Do not chase volume at the expense of clarity. For search and AI, the reusable, well-structured answer matters more than posting frequency alone.

## Example Recording Prompts

Use the situation-first prompt format in `content/social/templates/situation-first-recording-batch.md`.

Examples of the right level of specificity:

- A couple five years from retirement has saved well, but still cannot tell whether they are actually ready to stop working.
- A high-earning executive with equity comp keeps making good income but feels like every financial decision creates three more decisions.
- A business owner had a profitable year, paid a painful tax bill, and quietly wonders whether the business is building personal wealth or just consuming attention.
- A family inherited money and feels pressure to make the right moves before they even understand what they own.
- A retiree is being told to do Roth conversions, but no one has connected the tax decision to Medicare, cash flow, and the investment plan.

## Website Conversion Pattern
Each article created from a video should include:
- Short direct answer near the top.
- David byline and credentials.
- Local or client-fit context when relevant.
- 3-5 internal links.
- Compliance-safe disclaimer.
- CTA to an Explore Call or relevant Keystone page.

## Flagship Lead-Magnet Directions

Build toward two primary referral/lead-magnet assets:

- Tax-smart retirement planning in Northeast Tennessee: retirement timing, income, Roth windows, Social Security, Medicare, investment risk, and tax sequencing.
- Business owner tax strategy and personal-wealth planning: owner pay, entity decisions, retirement plans, estimated taxes, cash flow, exit readiness, and building wealth outside the business.

Secondary resources can exist, but the first two flagship assets should reinforce the primary lanes before adding more audience-specific downloads.
