Who this is actually for.

Families and business owners facing financial decisions that have started to overlap. Retirement, taxes, investments, business cash flow, and family planning are no longer separate conversations.

The decisions we help with

Retirement income timing

When to start drawing, from where, and in what order.

Tax strategy

Roth conversions, charitable timing, entity questions, and year-end moves that need to fit the plan.

Business owner coordination

Owner compensation, cash flow, retirement plans, estimated taxes, and the bridge from business profit to personal wealth.

Estate and family coordination

Making sure documents, beneficiaries, intentions, and future family decisions actually agree.

Investments serving the plan

Allocation, risk, and account structure based on what the money needs to do.

Questions that tend to sound like yours

We're about five years from retirement and I think we're in good shape, but I honestly don't know how to turn what we've saved into an income we can count on.

The business is doing well, but the tax bills, retirement plan, cash reserves, and what we're actually building for our household all feel disconnected.

My mom passed and I've inherited more than I expected. I don't want to make a decision I'll regret just because I didn't know what I didn't know.

What Keystone isn't

Not a quick answer to a single question. Not a product conversation. And not, by default, an ongoing management relationship. It's a defined engagement with a beginning and an end.

Section 03 · The Explore Call

What to expect on the Explore Call.

Short. Honest. Low stakes. A conversation, not a presentation. You don't need to arrive prepared, and nothing is expected of you afterward.

Length

Roughly 15 minutes

Long enough to get the picture, short enough that it fits between the rest of your day.

Format

Phone

Usually the easiest way to see whether going further makes sense.

What to bring

Nothing at all

No financial documents. No statements. No spreadsheets. We don't need them yet, and the call doesn't depend on them.

Prep required

None

Showing up is enough. If something specific is on your mind, you can bring it, but the conversation works either way.

There is no pressure to move forward after the call. If it's a fit, we'll talk about next steps. If it isn't, I'll tell you that directly, and point you toward whatever looks more useful. Either ending is a good one.

A short note
Section 05 · In closing

Why I do this work.

The thing I see most often, and the thing that's hardest to watch, is successful people reaching an important financial chapter without the kind of clarity they've earned. They've done the saving. They've built the business. They've made responsible decisions for years. And then they reach the moment where all of that is supposed to turn into a life, a plan, or a next chapter, and nobody has sat them down and shown them how.

So they guess. Some guess low and under-live the plan. Others guess high and run out of room later. Business owners can build a strong company and still not be sure it is turning into personal wealth in the smartest way. Families can inherit money, sell a business, or approach retirement and still feel like the important pieces are being handled in separate rooms.

What gets in the way, usually, is that the pieces that matter most to the decision - income, the plan, the investments, the taxes, the business, and the estate picture - almost never get looked at together. Most of the industry lets the investments lead, hopes the math works, and treats tax as something to react to in April.

My work is the other way around. Start with the plan. Let the plan tell us what the investments need to do. Coordinate the tax strategy so it's a lever we use rather than a cost we absorb. That sequence sounds obvious when you say it. It's also genuinely rare.

That's the work. I'm glad you're thinking about it, and I'm looking forward to talking.
