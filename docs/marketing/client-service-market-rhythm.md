# Client Service Market Rhythm

Last updated: 2026-06-17

This file connects Talley Wealth's client service model to marketing strategy. It explains what we actually help each major service rhythm with and when that work naturally shows up during the year.

## Shared Operating Model

- Operational service rhythms: Retirees and retirement-transition households, Business owners, High earners and accumulators.
- Public center of gravity now emphasizes two core client situations: tax-smart retirement planning and business-owner profit-to-wealth planning. Roth conversion and retirement tax-window planning is a major retirement entry point, not a third public market.
- High earners and accumulators are a real service-model category, but should usually appear on central marketing pages as a supporting fit or nested entry point rather than a third equal homepage lane.
- Client-facing asset: Your Talley Wealth Annual Planning Rhythm.
- Core annual meeting: Annual Planning Meeting.
- Second planned ongoing-advisory meeting: Strategy Session.
- The page should make work visible without making meetings feel like the product.
- The four recurring planning windows are Tax Season Review, Planning Priorities, Implementation & Alignment, and Year-End Strategy.

## Marketing Use

- Use this to align website pages, email topics, seminar ideas, referral language, and CRM/client-service tags.
- Each service rhythm sees money through a different lens, so the same planning rhythm should be explained through the problems they actually feel.
- Content should sound practical and connected to decisions, not like a generic wealth-management menu.
- Do not turn these three service rhythms into a flat public target-market list. Central website pages should preserve the focused public center of gravity, while entry-point pages can speak directly to more specific situations.

## Entry-Point Map

Specific pages, guides, seminars, and campaigns should route into one of the service rhythms below unless they are intentionally cross-lens.

**Retirement / retiree entry points:** pre-retirees asking "can I retire?", already retired households, employer-benefit and pension decisions, Roth conversions, withdrawal order, Medicare/IRMAA, Social Security, surviving-spouse readiness, estate organization.

**Business owner entry points:** business-owner tax strategy, reasonable compensation, owner pay, retirement plan design, business/household cash flow, entity and deduction planning, succession, sale, exit, continuity, practice owners.

**High earner / accumulator entry points:** executives, equity compensation, physicians and healthcare professionals, bonuses, deferred compensation, benefits, open enrollment, HSA/FSA, insurance, college funding, tax-efficient saving, cash deployment.

**Cross-lens entry points:** inherited wealth during a retirement transition, business sale plus estate planning, divorce intersecting with retirement or ownership, multigenerational family planning.

## Retirees & retirement-transition households

**Lens:** They are trying to turn assets into a durable life plan. The emotional center is confidence, continuity, and not making a costly tax or income mistake in retirement.

**What we help with:**

- Retirement income and withdrawal strategy
- Roth conversion, RMD, QCD, withholding, Medicare, and IRMAA planning
- Portfolio alignment for income needs and downside tolerance
- Estate organization, beneficiaries, key contacts, and surviving-spouse readiness

**Annual rhythm:**

- February-April: Tax Season Review
- Spring / early summer: Planning Priorities - Annual Planning Meeting
- Summer-fall: Implementation & Alignment
- October-December: Year-End Strategy - Strategy Session

## Business owners

**Lens:** They are trying to make business success translate into personal wealth, resilience, and optionality. The emotional center is reducing financial dependence on the business while keeping tax and business decisions connected.

**What we help with:**

- Business and household cash-flow integration
- Owner compensation, entity, retirement plan, estimated tax, and deduction planning
- Risk, continuity, key-person, estate, and buy-sell coordination
- Succession, sale, exit, and personal wealth independence planning

**Annual rhythm:**

- February-April: Tax Season Review
- Spring / early summer: Planning Priorities - Annual Planning Meeting
- Summer-fall: Implementation & Alignment
- October-December: Year-End Strategy - Strategy Session

## High earners & accumulators

**Lens:** They are trying to convert high income into lasting wealth without letting taxes, benefits, debt, equity compensation, or lifestyle decisions pull the plan in different directions.

**What we help with:**

- Savings rate, cash flow, liquidity, debt, and cash deployment
- Tax planning around income, withholding, backdoor Roth, charitable giving, and equity compensation
- Benefits, open enrollment, HSA/FSA, insurance, and family protection
- College, estate basics, investment allocation, and major life-goal funding

**Annual rhythm:**

- February-April: Tax Season Review
- Spring / early summer: Planning Priorities - Annual Planning Meeting
- Summer-fall: Implementation & Alignment
- October-December: Year-End Strategy - Strategy Session
